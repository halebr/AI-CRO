You have now completed your analysis of this business — strategy memos, playbooks, unit economics, experiments, and key debates. Now I need you to produce two synthesis documents designed to be fed into Google NotebookLM for generating presentations and audio podcasts.

These documents must be completely self-contained. A reader (or an AI) with zero prior context should be able to read either document and fully understand the business, its trajectory, and its strategic position.

## Document 1: "The [Company] Story"

Write a compelling, narrative-driven account of this company from founding to present day. Think longform journalism — the kind of piece you'd read in The Information or a deep Bloomberg feature. Structure it as a story with chapters, not a report with bullet points.

Include:
- **The broken industry** — What's wrong with this market? Why does it exist this way? Make the reader feel the pain.
- **The founding team** — Who are these people? What did they do before? Why are they credible for this specific problem?
- **The product** — What did they actually build? Explain it concretely, with numbers. Don't say "AI-powered platform" — say what it does, how fast, at what cost, compared to what alternative.
- **The growth story in chapters** — Break the company's history into distinct eras. Each chapter should have a clear arc: what was the thesis, what happened, what changed.
- **The market context** — What macro forces are making this possible right now? Why couldn't this company have existed five years ago?
- **The competitive landscape** — Who else is trying to solve this? Why haven't they? Be specific about competitors — names, funding, revenue, model, and why they fall short.
- **The unit economics** — Explain how the business actually makes money. Don't just cite margin percentages — walk through a single transaction end to end.
- **The crisis or inflection point** — What is the central tension right now? What could go wrong? Be honest and specific.
- **The path forward** — What does the future look like if they execute? What are the big strategic bets?
- **What makes this unusual** — Why should someone care about this company specifically? What's the thing that makes you lean forward?

Write in a confident, authoritative voice. Use specific numbers everywhere — revenue figures, growth rates, costs, market sizes. Avoid hedge words. Make assertions and back them up. The goal is a document that makes someone say "I need to learn more about this company."

Target length: 3,000-5,000 words.

## Document 2: "Key Debates"

Write a structured exploration of the hardest strategic questions facing this business — the questions where smart, informed people genuinely disagree.

For each debate, use this exact structure:
- **The question** — framed as a genuine tension, not a strawman
- **The bull case** — the strongest version of one side, with a memorable quote or framing that captures the essence
- **The bear case** — the strongest version of the other side, equally compelling
- **The evidence** — specific data points that inform both sides (numbers, not narratives)
- **What would resolve it** — the specific data, experiment, or milestone that would settle the debate, and by when

Rules:
- Each side must be genuinely persuasive. If you can't write a compelling bear case, the debate isn't hard enough to include.
- Use real numbers from the analysis. No vague references to "strong growth" or "potential risks."
- Include 5-7 debates, covering: growth vs. quality tradeoffs, valuation/outcome scenarios, business model questions, concentration risks, pricing strategy, and long-term vision.
- End with a "consensus view" that synthesizes the debates into an overall assessment with explicit probability-weighted scenarios.

Target length: 3,000-5,000 words.

## Important

Both documents will be used as source material for NotebookLM to generate a presentation and an AI-generated podcast. This means:
- Every claim must be backed by a specific number or fact — NotebookLM will cite them
- The writing must flow narratively — NotebookLM's audio output mirrors the source tone
- No bullet-point-only sections — use full prose with bullets only for evidence lists
- Include memorable, quotable lines — these become the hooks in generated content
